📌 Today's Key Signals
⚠️ ISM Prices Index Surges to 78.3 — Highest Since Jun 2022
ISM Manufacturing PMI held at 52.7 (expansion), but the Prices Index surged +19.3pp over 2 months to 78.3. 17 of 18 industries reported rising costs. Iran war-driven oil shock is pushing input costs sharply higher, threatening corporate margins even as output expands. Stagflation risk is the dominant theme.
📉 February NFP: -92K — First Negative Print Since COVID
Nonfarm payrolls contracted by 92,000 in February vs. +126K prior. Unemployment rose to 4.4%. ADP for March came in at only +62K, suggesting Friday's NFP (Apr 3) may also disappoint. The labor market is cracking under rate pressure and geopolitical uncertainty.
🛢️ WTI Crude at $105 — Strait of Hormuz Shipping Down 90%
The Iran conflict (started Feb 27) has disrupted 20% of global oil transit through Hormuz. WTI surged from ~$69 to $105 in one month. EIA forecasts prices may ease below $80 by Q3 if conflict doesn't escalate, but risk of $120+ spike remains. Energy cost pass-through is the primary inflation catalyst.

The economy is at a critical inflection point. Stagflation signals are strengthening: manufacturing input costs are surging (ISM Prices 78.3) while the labor market is contracting (NFP -92K). The Fed is trapped — Core PCE at 3.06% prevents rate cuts, but weakening employment argues for easing. The Iran war's oil supply disruption is the primary catalyst, and resolution timing is the key variable. With Fear & Greed at 17 (Extreme Fear), contrarian history suggests this may be a 6-month buying opportunity, but only for investors with strong conviction and long horizons.
